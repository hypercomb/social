Programs · All of us

Belonging, built on purpose.

The widest circle. All of us is where relating well becomes a way a whole community holds each other — neighbours, strangers, generations, all practising the same thing in the same place.

Social neuroscience suggests we’re wired for connection — that the need to belong runs nearly as deep as the need for food or water. Yet belonging rarely just happens; it’s something a community builds, on purpose, in a real place. “All of us” is the work of making a room where the regular and the newcomer, the settled and the searching, all have a seat.

In practice it’s shared meals where you can’t tell who’s paying full price and who isn’t; circles open to the whole street; seasonal gatherings that mark time together. The aim isn’t a perfect community — it’s a community that keeps choosing each other.

What it can look like

Shared tables

Meals where everyone’s welcome and no one is singled out for what they can give.

Open circles

Gatherings the whole neighbourhood can walk into — every voice heard, every seat equal.

Marking time together

Seasons, milestones, griefs and celebrations — held in company instead of alone.

Welcoming newcomers

A community that turns toward the person who just walked in, and is glad they did.

A community needs a place to gather.

Humanity Centres in British Columbia gather on the traditional, ancestral, and unceded territories of the Coast Salish peoples — including the Snuneymuxw (Nanaimo); the Semiahmoo, Katzie, and Kwantlen (Surrey); and the Musqueam, Squamish, and Tsleil-Waututh (Vancouver / South False Creek) — who have cared for these lands since time immemorial.
