Practitioners

The people who hold the room.

A welcome doesn’t run itself. Practitioners are the people who tend the space, hold the circle, and make the welcome real — facilitators, hosts, and guides who’ve practised relating well enough to help others practise too.

Holding a good circle is a craft. It takes someone who can keep a space safe without controlling it, who can listen for what’s underneath, who can let silence do its work and step in when repair is needed. Our practitioners come from many backgrounds — facilitation, care, counselling, community work, the contemplative traditions — united by a practice of presence.

We’re gathering this circle of practitioners now, beginning on the West Coast. If holding space is your work or your calling, we’d love to meet you.

What a practitioner brings

Presence

The ability to be genuinely with people — grounded, unhurried, and real.

Holding the circle

Keeping a space safe and alive: shared agreements, every voice heard, no one steamrolled.

Skill in repair

Helping people name ruptures and mend them — the heart of relational practice.

Humility

A practitioner is a guide, not a guru. The community is the hero; we just hold the room.

Humanity Centres in British Columbia gather on the traditional, ancestral, and unceded territories of the Coast Salish peoples — including the Snuneymuxw (Nanaimo); the Semiahmoo, Katzie, and Kwantlen (Surrey); and the Musqueam, Squamish, and Tsleil-Waututh (Vancouver / South False Creek) — who have cared for these lands since time immemorial. We hold these relationships with gratitude and responsibility, as the first relationship we tend.
